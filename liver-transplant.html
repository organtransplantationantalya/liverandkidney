About the Procedure

When Is a Liver Transplant Needed?

Liver transplantation is recommended for end-stage liver disease when other treatments are no longer effective. It can save the lives of patients with cirrhosis, liver failure, or certain types of liver cancer.

  • Liver cirrhosis (alcoholic, viral, autoimmune)
  • Acute liver failure
  • Hepatocellular carcinoma
  • Metabolic liver diseases
  • Biliary atresia (in children)
Types of Transplant

Living Donor & Cadaveric Transplant

Living Donor

A portion of a healthy donor's liver (usually a relative) is transplanted to the recipient. Both livers regenerate to near-normal size within weeks.

Cadaveric Transplant

Liver from a deceased donor with confirmed brain death. The patient is placed on the national waiting list, and we ensure 24/7 emergency readiness.
